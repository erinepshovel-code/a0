The Interdependent Way

[Theory] A sociopolitical framework for competent interactions amid technological and institutional challenges.

Humanity faces risks from incompetence and dishonesty in authority. This proposes necessities for survival through interdependence.

Citations: Natural law (Locke, 1689); Human rights (UN, 1948).
